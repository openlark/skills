## 1. Sales Script Design

**Objective**: Embed psychological principles into scripts to enhance persuasiveness and closing rates.

### Core Principle Combinations

| Stage | Principle | Script Strategy |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Icebreaker | Reciprocity Principle | Provide valuable information/services for free |
| Needs Diagnosis | Framing Effect | Use question framing to guide customers to articulate their own pain points |
| Solution Presentation | Price Anchoring | Present the highest tier first → then push the recommended solution |
| Objection Handling | Loss Aversion | Emphasize "the loss of not changing" rather than "the benefits of changing" |
| Closing | Scarcity Effect | Limited time, limited quantity, limited spots |
| Post-Sale | Peak-End Rule | Value-added service + warm closing |

### Script Templates

```
Icebreaker (Reciprocity):
"Mr. Zhang, I've compiled a trend report on XX for your industry—no product promotion involved, purely for sharing."

Needs Diagnosis (Framing Effect):
"How much time does your team currently spend on XX? If you could save 2 days a week, what would you most want to use them for?"

Solution Presentation (Price Anchoring):
"This is the flagship plan with full XX feature coverage, at an annual investment of XX million."
→ After customer reaction →
"Actually, based on your needs, the standard version is sufficient—80% feature coverage at half the investment."

Objection Handling (Loss Aversion):
"I understand your concerns. But let's do a calculation: your current XX process loses XX efficiency per cycle, totaling about XX per month. The cost of maintaining the status quo may be higher than the cost of change."

Closing (Scarcity Effect):
"I have 3 spots at this price; they'll be gone after tomorrow's adjustment. You don't need to decide now, but I'll lock in a spot for you."
```

## 2. Pricing Strategy Formulation

**Objective**: Design a pricing structure that makes consumers "voluntarily" choose higher-priced tiers/combinations.

### Four-Step Pricing Method

**Step 1: Anchor (Price Anchoring)**
- Display the highest price point first in the price list
- All figures appear "lowered" against the anchor
- Strategy: The premium product is not for selling; it's there to help the mid-tier sell better

**Step 2: Three-Tier Architecture (Price Anchoring + Compromise Effect)**

```
Basic Plan (¥X) — Limited features, creates a comparison baseline
Recommended Plan (¥Y) — Best value for money, guides choice
Flagship Plan (¥Z) — Anchor role, elevates overall pricing perception
```

- Recommended plan pricing = Flagship × 60-70%
- Price gap between Basic and Recommended > Price gap between Recommended and Flagship (guides upward migration)

**Step 3: Frame Reframing (Framing Effect)**
- Annual fee → "Only XX per day"
- One-time investment → "Equivalent to XX cups of coffee"
- Show "the hidden cost of not using XX" (Loss Aversion)

**Step 4: Payment Pain Reduction**
- Installment payments reduce immediate pain (Instant Gratification)
- Complimentary accessories/services (Reciprocity Principle)

### Pricing Self-Check Checklist

- [ ] Is there an "anchor product" (not measured by sales, only for anchoring)?
- [ ] Is the recommended product in the "compromise position"?
- [ ] Does the price expression use "unit breakdown" (per day/per use)?
- [ ] Is "the cost of not using" displayed?
- [ ] Is there a "risk-free commitment" (refund/return)?

## 3. Advertising Copywriting

**Objective**: Drive the conversion power of every word using psychological principles.

### Copywriting Psychology Formula

```
Attention = Scarcity + Instant Gratification (limited time/quantity/immediate)
Trust = Social Proof + Authority Effect (data/reviews/certifications)
Desire = Loss Aversion + Framing Effect (fear of missing out/shift perspective)
Action = Reciprocity + Instant Gratification (give first/get immediately)
```

### Headline Template Library

**Scarcity Type**:
- "Last 24 Hours | Miss today's discount, wait another year"
- "Limited to 300 Copies | 268 Already Claimed"

**Social Proof Type**:
- "The Choice of 1 Million Users | Why did they choose XX?"
- "The Method Used by the TOP 10 in the XX Industry"

**Loss Aversion Type**:
- "If you don't change these 3 habits, you'll overspend by XX every year"
- "Your competitors are already using this solution"

**Framing Effect Type**:
- "For just ¥5 a day, elevate your child's English beyond 90% of their peers"
- "It's not expensive; you just haven't calculated the cost of not using it"

### Copywriting Structure (AIDA + Psychology)

```
Attention (Scarcity/Social Proof): Create attention and urgency
Interest (Social Proof/Authority): Data and testimonials build credibility
Desire (Loss Aversion/Framing): Depict the transformation after owning or the loss of not using
Action (Reciprocity/Instant Gratification): Offer benefits first, then give a call to action
```

## 4. Product Page Optimization

**Objective**: Optimize e-commerce product detail pages/landing pages using psychological principles to improve browse-to-conversion efficiency.

### Golden Page Layout

| Position | Content | Principle |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Hero Section | Core selling point + limited-time label | Scarcity + Instant Gratification |
| Second Section | Pain point scenario description | Loss Aversion + Framing Effect |
| Third Section | User reviews + sales data | Social Proof |
| Fourth Section | Product comparison (competitor vs. us) | Price Anchoring |
| Fifth Section | Authority endorsement (certifications/experts/media) | Authority Effect |
| Sixth Section | Gifts/extra benefits | Reciprocity Principle |
| Bottom Section | Urgency + call to action | Scarcity + Instant Gratification |

### Detail Page Element Checklist

**Trust Signals**:
- Star ratings + number of reviews
- Cumulative sales/user count
- Real-time purchase pop-up notifications ("XX just placed an order")
- Authority certification badges

**Urgency Signals**:
- Inventory countdown/progress bar
- Limited-time discount countdown
- "XX people are currently viewing"

**Value Signals**:
- Original price strikethrough comparison
- Total value of bundled gifts
- Service/after-sales commitment icons

**Action Signals**:
- Comparison table (us vs. competitors) — Anchoring
- Use case scenario visuals — Endowment Effect
- User case study details — Social Proof

## 5. Promotional Campaign Planning

**Objective**: Design promotional campaigns that maximize perceived value, rather than simply discounting.

### Promotional Psychology Design Principles

1. **Discounts need a "reason"**: Anniversary sales > random discounts (positive use of the Scarcity Effect)
2. **Threshold restrictions**: "Members only," "First order only" — Exclusivity = Scarcity
3. **Tiered discounts**: ¥20 off ¥199 / ¥60 off ¥399 / ¥100 off ¥599 — Sunk Cost guidance ("just a little more to reach the next tier")
4. **Gift strategy**: "Buy A, get B free; B is worth ¥299" — Reciprocity + Anchoring
5. **Social virality**: "Invite friends; each gets XX" — Social Proof + Reciprocity

### Five Promotional Psychology Models

| Type | Core Principle | Design Essentials |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Flash Sale | Scarcity + Instant Gratification | Countdown + visible inventory + XX people already participating |
| Threshold Discount | Sunk Cost + Anchoring | Differentiated tier settings to guide add-on purchases to the next tier |
| Gift with Purchase | Reciprocity + Anchoring | Gift price clearly labeled to maximize perceived value |
| Group Buy | Social Proof + Social Identity | Visible number of group participants, friend participation visible |
| Pre-Sale | Scarcity + Endowment Effect | Limited numbered editions, exclusive early-bird benefits |

### Promotional Launch Checklist

- Are scarcity signals sufficient? (Inventory/Time/Spots)
- Is social proof visible? (Participant count/Sales volume/Reviews)
- Is there an anchoring reference? (Original price/Gift value)
- Are barriers to action reduced? (Refund guarantee/No-questions-asked returns)

## 6. Customer Needs Analysis

**Objective**: Use psychology to uncover customers' genuine needs (beyond surface-level expressions) and guide product and marketing efforts.

### Four-Level Needs Discovery

```
Level 1 (Surface): "I want a cheaper price"
     ↓ Framing Effect: Reframe the question from a different angle
Level 2 (Contextual): "I don't think this product is worth the price"
     ↓ Loss Aversion: Calculate the hidden costs
Level 3 (Emotional): "I'm worried I'll spend money and see no results"
     ↓ Social Proof: Show outcomes from similar users
Level 4 (Psychological): "I need proof that this decision is the right one"
     ↓ Authority + Reciprocity: Provide third-party validation + additional value
```

### Customer Psychological Profile Dimensions

| Dimension | Probing Question | Corresponding Psychological Strategy |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Risk Perception | What is the customer most worried about going wrong? | Social Proof + Authority Endorsement |
| Reference System | What/who is the customer comparing against? | Re-anchor + Frame Shift |
| Decision Barriers | What missing information is causing hesitation? | Reciprocity Principle (provide information first) |
| Identity | Who does the customer want/not want to be? | Bandwagon Effect (choices of similar people) |
| Sunk Cost | How much has the customer invested in the old solution? | Loss Aversion (calculate the loss of continuing) |

### Needs Analysis Scripts

- "If XX could be solved, how much value do you think that would create?" → Framing Effect to guide self-discovery
- "Your peers in XX are already using this solution; their XX metric improved by XX%" → Social Proof + Authority
- "If you maintain the status quo, how much loss do you estimate over the next six months?" → Loss Aversion

## 7. Brand Trust Building

**Objective**: Systematically apply psychological principles to build long-term consumer trust in the brand.

### Trust Building Pyramid

```
              ┌──────────────────┐
              │ Emotional Bond   │ ← Peak-End Rule + Endowment Effect
              ├──────────────────┤
              │ Ongoing Validation│ ← Social Proof (continuous)
              ├──────────────────┤
              │ Authority Backing │ ← Authority Effect
              ├──────────────────┤
              │ Reciprocity First │ ← Reciprocity Principle
              └──────────────────┘
```

### Four Stages of Trust Building

**Stage 1: Reciprocity First (Reducing Guard)**
- Provide valuable content for free without asking for anything in return
- Strategy: Industry whitepapers, practical tools, free diagnostics
- KPI: Content downloads/usage count

**Stage 2: Authority Backing (Building Credibility)**
- Obtain verifiable third-party certifications and endorsements
- Strategy: Patents/certifications/awards/media mentions/expert recommendations
- KPI: Coverage and awareness of endorsements

**Stage 3: Social Proof (Ongoing Validation)**
- Accumulate authentic user reviews and case studies
- Strategy: User stories, data transparency, client logo wall
- KPI: Number of reviews, number of case studies, user NPS

**Stage 4: Emotional Bond (Peak-End Experience)**
- Create peak moments and perfect endings at key touchpoints
- Strategy: Unboxing experience, after-sales surprises, annual rewards
- KPI: Repurchase rate, referral rate

### Trust Crisis Response

| Crisis Type | Wrong Response | Psychologically Correct Response |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------------------|
| Product quality issues | Avoid/deflect | Reciprocity First (overcompensate) + Social Proof (publicly disclose rectification progress) |
| False advertising allegations | Legalistic defense | Authority Validation (invite third-party inspection) + Immediate transparency |
| Negative sentiment spreading | Delete posts/silence | Peak-End Rule (sincere apology + corrective action + closing with added benefits) |

```
         Gratifi-                               cation   Rule
         cation
```

### Conversion Barriers × Psychological Solutions

| Conversion Barrier | User Psychology | Psychological Solution |
|--------------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Price hesitation | "It's too expensive" | Anchoring + Framing (unit breakdown) + Loss Aversion (cost of not using) |
| Lack of trust | "Is this a scam?" | Social Proof + Authority + Reciprocity (provide value first) |
| Decision difficulty | "Which one to choose?" | Price Anchoring (recommended tag) + Social Proof (best-seller label) |
| Payment resistance | "Do I have to pay now?" | Instant Gratification (installments/cash on delivery) + Loss Aversion (refund guarantee) |
| Churn recovery | "Forget it, I'm not buying" | Reciprocity (retention coupon) + Scarcity (limited-time return offer) |

### Page-Level Optimization Checklist

- [ ] Is the value proposition clearly communicated within 3 seconds? (Framing Effect)
- [ ] Are there urgency signals? (Scarcity Effect: countdown/inventory/limited quantity)
- [ ] Are there social proof signals? (Reviews, sales volume, real-time activity)
- [ ] Is there authority backing? (Certification badges, expert recommendations, media mentions)
- [ ] Is there a commitment guarantee? (Refund/return/warranty = Loss Aversion relief)
- [ ] Does the CTA button reflect instant gratification? ("Get Now" > "Buy Now")
- [ ] Is the price anchored? (Original price/competitor price/package comparison)
- [ ] Are there additional gifts/benefits? (Reciprocity Principle drives the final push)
