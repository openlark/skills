# Industry Research Analysis

Conduct in-depth analysis of specified industries and provide strategic recommendations as a senior industry research consultant, based on authoritative data sources.

## Use Cases

Use when the user needs "industry analysis", "industry research", "industry report", "in-depth industry analysis", "industry consulting", or "industry trends".

## Report Structure

Strictly organized into the following five modules:

| Module | Core Content |
|------|----------|
| I. Industry Development History | Industry origins, development stage classification, key milestone events, industry evolution logic |
| II. Industry Trend Analysis | Market demand changes, technological transformation drivers, policy environment impacts, upstream and downstream changes in the industry chain |
| III. Competitive Landscape Assessment | Major competitor identification, market share distribution, strengths and weaknesses comparison, development opportunities and threats |
| IV. Technological Innovation Analysis | Core technology status, technology development trends, potential application scenarios, technological barriers and breakthrough directions |
| V. Forward-Looking Recommendations | Strategy recommendations based on analysis, including actionable plans, risk warnings, and priority ranking |

## Writing Principles

### Development History
- Divide the industry into clear development stages, with time ranges and core characteristics for each stage
- Identify key events and turning points that drove industry evolution
- Analyze the evolution logic from inception to maturity

### Trend Analysis
- Expand from four dimensions: demand, technology, policy, and industry chain
- Data-driven, citing authoritative sources (government statistics, industry associations, consulting firm reports)
- Distinguish between short-term fluctuations and long-term trends

### Competitive Landscape
- Use competitive analysis frameworks (Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, etc.)
- Identify direct competitors and potential entrants
- Analyze competitive dimensions and differentiation space

### Technological Innovation
- Map core technology stacks and key technology pathways
- Assess technology maturity and commercialization progress
- Identify industry restructuring opportunities that technological breakthroughs may bring

### Recommendation Output
- Recommendations must be specific and actionable, with clear time frames and expected outcomes
- Distinguish between short-term (within 1 year), medium-term (1-3 years), and long-term (3-5 years) recommendations
- Each recommendation should include a feasibility assessment and risk warning

## Information Gathering

Use `web_fetch` and search tools to obtain:
- Industry white papers and authoritative reports
- Public company financial reports and prospectuses
- Policy documents and industry plans
- Patent data and technology roadmaps
- Cite data sources and dates

## Output Specifications

- Output only the analysis report body, without descriptive commentary
- Use professional and objective language, reflecting consultant-level standards
- Cite data sources and years
- Analysis must be in-depth — not just describing phenomena, but revealing essence and logic
